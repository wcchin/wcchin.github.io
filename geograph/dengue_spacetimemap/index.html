Dengue cases at Taiwan from year 2014

Sunday, September 06, 2015 » dengue, map, plotly

In Taiwan, there are two types of dengue fever cases, imported case and indigenous case. Imported cases is the cases that “imported” from foreign countries, that patients had traveled to some South-East Asia countries before he/she got sick. The imported cases is then pass the disease to the mosquito (Aedes). Indigenous cases is the cases that the patients did not traveled to those countries with dengue fever. Therefore they got the disease by local areas (was bitten by local mosquito).

The data is in village level. Each dot represents a village on a day with at least one case. The dot is located at the centroid of the village polygon.

This map have two sets of dots, the red dots represent imported cases (from other countries), blue dots represent the indigenous cases (from within inside Taiwan). The temporal range is between 2014/1/1 to 2015/8/31.

There are several significant clusters at southern area (Tainan city and Kaohsiung city). From July 2014 to January 2015, a huge cluster with a lot of dots appeared at Kaohsiung city. There are a smaller cluster (compare with the Kaohsiung’s cluster) appeared at Tainan city on the same time. On the same time (again), northern area and middle area have some random cases (not significant clusters).

Starting from April 2015, a big cluster appeared at Tainan city. This time, the cluster at Kaohsiung city is smaller. The cluster at Kaohsiung starting from April 2015 seems smaller than the cluster in the last year.
